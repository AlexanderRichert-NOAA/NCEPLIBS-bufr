2 ## User Guide
3 @brief Comprehensive discussion on how to incorporate the library within user application programs.
4 
5 @anchor manual
6 The BUFR format is officially documented in
7 [WMO Manual 306, Volume I.2](https://library.wmo.int/index.php?lvl=notice_display&id=10684#.X68yu8hKiUn).
8 The latest edition of BUFR is edition 4, although edition 3 is still in use in many parts of the world.
9 
10 @anchor wmomstab
11 BUFR is a table-driven format, meaning
12 that new parameters can be encoded and decoded by adding them to tables which are read in by the software,
13 rather than having to modify the software itself. To that end, WMO periodically releases
14 new versions of their
15 [official WMO master BUFR tables](https://community.wmo.int/activity-areas/wmo-codes/manual-codes/latest-version),
16 to facilitate the continued exchange of meteorological data throughout the global community.
17 
18 NCEPLIBS-bufr (also often referred to as BUFRLIB) is a software library that can read (decode) and
19 write (encode) data in both edition 3 and edition 4 of BUFR. It consists of more than 300 different subroutines and
20 functions; however, a typical application program will never directly call more than 10 to 20 of them, and
21 the rest are lower-level routines that the library uses to accomplish various underlying tasks, and which
22 can therefore be considered as "black box" from a user perspective.
23 
24 Whenever new versions of the official WMO master BUFR tables are released by WMO (as noted above),
25 they are downloaded and reformatted as new [NCEPLIBS-bufr master BUFR tables](@ref dfbfmstab) for use with the
26 software and distributed with the next release of the library. However, users can also generate their own custom
27 [NCEPLIBS-bufr DX BUFR tables](@ref dfbftab) for use with the software as needed.
28 
29 More details are available throughout the documentation, including sample utilities which demonstrate
30 how to use many of the various library subroutines and functions to accomplish different tasks.
31 
32 @anchor hierarchy
33 For now, it's important to understand the following hierarchy:
34 
35 1. Any BUFR file can consist of one or more BUFR messages.
36 2. Any BUFR message can consist of one or more BUFR data subsets, which are akin to reports from individual
37 observational stations at a particular time and location.
38 3. Any BUFR data subset can consist of one or more BUFR data descriptors and corresponding data values.
39 
40 This in turn allows many of the most commonly used library subroutines and functions to be grouped accordingly,
41 based on which level of the hierarchy they operate at, and whether for reading/decoding BUFR data or
42 writing/encoding BUFR data:
